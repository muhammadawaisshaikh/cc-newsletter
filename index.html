A New Organizational Structure: From Traditional to Matrix

Earlier this year, Creative Chaos evolved from a traditional hierarchy to an agile matrix structure.The shift was a result of inefficiencies caused by the previous structure, which led to hierarchical rigidity and internal silos; coordination between departments was poor and affected the quality of project delivery. After extensive research, discussion, and evaluation of options best suited forthe company, a unanimous decision was taken to introduce the matrix structure.The matrix structure introduces terms such as squads, chapters, chapter leads, project owners, coaches and squad members. The idea is to create squads for each project that are independently responsible for the end to end delivery of a project. This will lead to better collaboration between squad members from different chapters, create better synergies and leadto quality product delivery. So let’s see how the new structure fares compared to the previous one and whether it will work for Creative Chaos in the long run.

Featured Project: Litterati

What is Litterati? Litterati is a social venture focused on environmental conservation through community-based litter collection. Through the app, users can track and geotag pieces of litter that they collect. This data is then analyzed to provide valuable insights into major sources of pollution, their effects, and the parties accountable for the litter.

Celebrating our associates’ achievements

It’s not every day that the world’s most celebrated tech company with military-grade security fails to spot a few bugs. Two of our associates, Shayan Hussaini and Arjun Bhatnagar, found a bug in Google’s AutoAL architecture. It took two weeks of continuous back and forth to get in touch with Google’s Chief Architect. The team at Google vehemently disagreed, but our associates weren’t ready to back off and pointed out the architectural flaws in an intense discussion. Finally, after a long-winded debate, Google acknowledged the bugs and appreciated the efforts of Shayan and Arjun.

Behance is an online platform to showcase your design portfolio. Each year, Behance organizes a design competition in Pakistan to highlight local talent. This time around, there were around 50 participants in various categories. The designs were evaluated by UI/UX experts from the local market through a voting system. One of our associates, Hunain Ahmed won the design competition and a silver Behance coin to commemorate his win. Behance selected Hunain’s portfolio on the basis of design story, aesthetics, and creativity.

Ignite is one of the leading advocates of startups in Pakistan. They recently organized NGIRI, a national competition, to judge the best Final Year Project of 2018. Projects were submitted from all over Pakistan, after which 43 were shortlisted and called to Islamabad. Projects were evaluated by 10 judges from multiple fields (IT, Entrepreneurship, Economics, and Investments). Bilal Aamir, one of our associates, also submitted his FYP and won the second runner up position with a cash prize. Bilal built a web application using deep learning to predict crypto-currency values in 15 minute intervals with tools tailored for both amateur and professional investors. The award was presented by the Minister of IT and the event was fully-funded by the government of Pakistan.
